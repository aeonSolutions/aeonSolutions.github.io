Cloudless Personal Health App

The solution is optimized for a cloudless decentralized and dummy-proof for everyday usage by any citizen on any health-related business or public institution:

Assumptions

  • It is assumed to be secure each device where a copy exists of the medical data. For instance, it is accepted IOS on the iPhone to be secure.
  • It is assumed to be secure any exchange platform for the medical data access (in any hospital for instance).
  • It Is assumed to be secure any terminal computer for local access to medical data (local 2 local access). For instance, in regions where there is no Internet connectivity or in the event of Internet failure.
  • In the event of an emergency, medical data needs to be accessible with just one click. For instance, an iris scan or fingerprint read.
  • Medical data records are the responsibility of a citizen. That is to say, if he/she forges medical data it won't be on his/her best interest.
  • In proved cases where the citizen is unable to handle adequately his/her medical records, a third person should be allowed to do it. If any, the public health system / private company should be paid by the state to provide such service free of charge.

Solution requirements

  • A health data transfer protocol that ensures privacy/secrecy of medical data across networks that can connect any device for that purpose.
  • A microservice to keep up to date the latest version all copies of the medical data of a given citizen any time a device with a copy of the data, connects to the health data protocol network. For instance, let's say I have 6 copies of my medical data on 6 different devices at home. Lately, I've been using the same device, my smartphone for instance, when visiting the doctor in the last couple of months. Naturally, this device has a more up to date record of my medical data. Other devices on plugging in into the health network need to update, upon request, to the latest data records. (is needed some sort of "tracking ID" for knowing what is the latest version of the medical data)
Medical data access needs to be made on a local secured network. Only if needed, accessed through the Internet. For instance, using RFID / NFC technology to do data access, for instance, in a doctor's office. Or the faster but proprietary transferJet technology for the same purpose.
